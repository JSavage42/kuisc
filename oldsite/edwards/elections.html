Matt Loo

Hello, my name is Matthew Loo. I am a senior in the BSIT program with over 4 years of professional experience in IT at various companies such as Dell Technologies, EMC, Coca-Cola, QTS Data Centers, and Kansas City Southern Railroad and am currently an Information Security Engineer at Wells Fargo Bank. My professional certifications include multiple Splunk administration and architectural certifications and multiple Cisco networking certifications as well. As a member of the KUISC “Jayhackers”, I have participated in two competitions, one of which as the team captain, both resulting in a third place finishes. As the current Chief Technology Officer of the Edwards Campus chapter, I have also assisted the club by providing expertise and instruction during hands-on labs and competition set-up phases as well as delivering presentations during meetings involving data analytics, machine learning, and automation and their involvement in security threat response. Additionally, in cooperation with my other group members (all of which are also nominated for officer positions this election), my capstone project will hopefully result in the second iteration of the “High Score” cyber defense competition, a KU hosted CDC designed to introduce local high school students to competitions. Outside of the Jayhackers, I have also participated in Splunk’s “Boss of the SOC”, a security threat hunting competition involving Splunk’s Enterprise Security and User Behavior Analytics platforms in conjunction with Phantom automation tools and also regularly participate in Wells Fargo security training competitions as well, most recently vs. the US Naval Academy. In my free time I enjoy exploring a wide range of security related concepts involving steganography, cryptography, penetration testing, and system hardening.
